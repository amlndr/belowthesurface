How We Build it: Simulating Overuse of Groundwater.

;

To raise awareness on groundwater overuse, we want people who interact with our artefact to be curious and know its impactful consequence to the earth.
By simulating water, we create a waving motion that is triggered by removing a cup of water and just imagine it. Imagine the 10 million people using groundwater everyday.

Below is the interaction chart:

Based on the chart above, we want our user to understand how their actions can have consequences towards nature. Although nature always evolve at a slower pace, humans make the change go at a faster rate. And that is what we want our user to feel, and be mindfully curious on how their action can impact nature and its surroundings, including humans.

To make the organic shape into place, we use 3 card boards, with the one in the middle have bits of its parts cut out to fit into the circle.

We also tested our electronics, using a pressure sensor and a grove motor shield with an arduino.

The pressure sensor and the grove motor shield each have their own arduino, connected with wires to communicate to each other. Where the grove motor shield is the client and the pressure sensor as the server.
below is the diagram on how it works.

Next we programmed the artefact so that when a person puts a cup on the pressure sensor, it can detect how much water is in the cup, and it will make the artefact to start moving in accordance to its weight. Where the less water, the faster the actuator moves.
